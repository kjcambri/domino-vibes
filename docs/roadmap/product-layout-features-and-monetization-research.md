# Product Layout, Player Features, and Monetization Research

This document turns market research into a Domino Vibes product model: what players should see, what features are worth building, and how the app can make money without becoming pay-to-win, gambling-coded, or hostile to casual players.

## Research Takeaways

Players in successful online board/card apps consistently respond to five things:

- Fast entry into a real match.
- Private tables with friends.
- Public lobbies that feel alive.
- Progression, stats, rankings, and achievements.
- Personal expression through avatars, table themes, emotes, and profile identity.

Competitor signals:

- Dominoes Jogatina advertises online/offline play, bots, friends, multiplayer, four modes, tile/table customization, stats, private matches, ads, in-app purchases, and no-ads subscriptions. Its App Store listing shows no-ads options at $1.99 weekly, $3.99 monthly, $29.99 annually, and a one-time remove-ads purchase at $4.99.
- Tableverse uses a familiar board-game platform split: free public lobbies and chat, paid private lobby creation, ranked matches, priority matchmaking, replays, and premium emotes at $6/month or $60/year.
- Newer social game products like Cadito emphasize a simple home flow: find strangers online, start private game, join with code, live chat/voice, then a recap memory.
- Research on multiplayer retention finds that achievement systems matter earlier in a player's lifecycle, while social features become more predictive of long-term retention for experienced players.
- Research on social-game features warns that presence and chat are common, but deeper player interaction is often underbuilt. Domino Vibes should use clubs, partners, rematches, recaps, and shared table identity to avoid being just "chat next to a board."

Policy and trust signals:

- Apple requires randomized paid virtual items to disclose odds before purchase, and requires real-money gaming apps to be licensed and geo-restricted.
- Google Play does not allow unlicensed apps to let users wager, stake, or use money or paid items to obtain prizes of real-world value.
- Domino Vibes should avoid real-money prizes, sweepstakes coins, wagering language, casino chips, buy-ins, prize pools, and any cash-equivalent currency.

## Recommended Product Shape

Domino Vibes should be a free-to-play social domino hall with premium membership, cosmetics, season passes, and optional ads. The app should monetize comfort, self-expression, hosting power, replay depth, and community status. It should not monetize legal moves, skill advantage, table odds, or access to fair competition.

## Best Layout Model

### 1. Home: Player Launchpad

The first logged-in screen should answer: "Where can I play right now?"

Layout:

- Continue Match banner at the top when the player has an active game.
- Quick Play button for the recommended mode.
- Friends/Private Table action beside Quick Play.
- Live Tables strip showing open seats and current active matches.
- Daily Challenge card.
- Recent Recap card from the last match.
- Profile progress row: level, streak, rank, season progress.

Why players will like it:

- They can start fast.
- They can rejoin instantly.
- They get a small reason to return daily.
- The app feels alive before they enter the lobby.

### 2. Lobby: Social Table Hall

The lobby should feel like a premium domino room, not a spreadsheet of rooms.

Layout:

- Mode tabs: Jamaican Cut Throat, Partners, Cuban, All Fives, More.
- Filters: public/private, ranked/casual, open seats, friends only.
- Table cards with mode, seats, target score, skill/rank band, current status, and host.
- Live chat or room feed in a side panel on desktop and a bottom tab on mobile.
- Friend activity: online now, invite, watch, rejoin.
- Featured Table / Weekly Challenge area.

Must-have features:

- Quick Match.
- Create Table.
- Join with Code.
- Invite Friend.
- Rematch from previous opponents.
- Spectate when allowed.

### 3. Table Room: Pre-Game Ritual

This is where players commit socially.

Layout:

- Seat grid with avatars, online/away state, team labels, and ready states.
- Table settings summary: mode, target score, teams, timer, privacy.
- Host controls.
- Chat.
- Start Game panel.
- House rules preview.

Best paid opportunity:

- Let everyone join private tables for free.
- Let free users create a limited number of private tables per day.
- Give members unlimited private table hosting, saved table presets, club tables, and custom table themes.

### 4. Game Room: Board-First, Social-Second

The current design direction is right: board first, side social/status panel, bottom hand rack.

Layout:

- Center: board with open ends, latest move highlight, and clear legal targets.
- Bottom: player's hand, legal move controls, pass/draw button.
- Top: current turn, score, round, mode, timer.
- Sides: opponents, team status, hand counts, pass/away indicators.
- Drawer/panel: chat, move log, score details, table reactions.
- Round-end overlay: winner, reason, score swing, key play, rematch/next-round action.

Player-loved details:

- Legal move glow.
- Satisfying tile slam, haptics, and sound pack options.
- Quick reactions after big plays.
- "Why can't I play?" helper when blocked.
- Move log that explains scoring.
- No hidden-hand leaks, ever.

### 5. Profile: Identity and Proof

Profiles are a major monetization surface because cosmetics matter only when other players see them.

Layout:

- Avatar, frame, title, club, country/island flag or region badge.
- Mode-specific rank cards.
- Win rate, streaks, blocked wins, partner record, All Fives points, Cuban team record.
- Favorite mode and table theme.
- Trophy wall and achievement badges.
- Match history and replay links.

Paid value:

- More profile cosmetics.
- Premium badges/titles.
- Extended match history.
- Advanced stat comparisons.
- Shareable recap cards.

### 6. Clubs / Crews

This should become the social spine after core gameplay is stable.

Layout:

- Club home: members online, club tables, weekly standings, announcements.
- Club table presets.
- Club chat.
- Club challenges and internal leaderboards.

Paid value:

- Free users can join clubs.
- Members can create clubs.
- Club owners can buy upgraded club identity packs: banner, table theme, crest, announcement tools.

## Recommended Monetization Model

### Free Tier

Purpose: Keep tables full and make the app easy to recommend.

Includes:

- Public tables.
- Core modes as they launch.
- Join private tables.
- Limited private table hosting, such as 3 created tables per day.
- Basic chat.
- Basic stats.
- Basic achievements.
- Limited match history.
- Rewarded ads for bonus cosmetic currency.
- Interstitial ads only between matches or after completed rounds, never during a turn.

### Domino Vibes Club Membership

Recommended price:

- $4.99/month.
- $39.99/year.
- Optional founder annual price during beta: $29.99/year.

Membership should include:

- No forced ads.
- Unlimited private table hosting.
- Saved table presets.
- Premium profile frame and member badge.
- Monthly cosmetic drop.
- Premium emotes and table reactions.
- Custom table felt and tile-back options.
- Extended match history and replay archive.
- Advanced stats by mode.
- Priority matchmaking during peak load, but not easier opponents.
- Club creation.
- Early access to new cosmetic themes and beta modes, with clear "testing" labels.

Why players would pay:

- Dominoes is social; hosting and identity are visible.
- Members get a better room, better memory, and better status.
- Players who organize games with friends get real utility.
- Competitive players want advanced stats and replays.
- Casual players may pay just to remove ads and personalize the table.

### One-Time Purchases

Recommended products:

- Remove forced ads forever: $9.99.
- Table theme packs: $2.99 to $7.99.
- Tile back packs: $1.99 to $4.99.
- Avatar/frame packs: $1.99 to $5.99.
- Sound pack / table ambience pack: $1.99 to $3.99.
- Shareable recap card styles: $1.99 to $3.99.

Guideline: direct purchase beats random loot boxes. If randomized packs are ever used, odds must be disclosed and the app must avoid casino-style framing.

### Seasonal Pass

Recommended price:

- $7.99 per 8-10 week season.
- Included or discounted for annual members.

Rewards:

- Cosmetic-only table themes.
- Tile backs.
- Avatar frames.
- Profile titles.
- Emotes.
- Recap card templates.
- Club banner accents.

Rules:

- No gameplay advantages.
- No paid-only ranked power.
- No paid access to better scoring.
- No pressure timers that hide the true price or trick renewals.

### Cosmetic Currency

Recommended model:

- Use one soft currency earned through play, achievements, daily challenges, and rewarded ads.
- Use direct cash purchases for premium cosmetic packs.
- Avoid a confusing paid currency at launch.
- Never let currency be cashed out, transferred, sold, wagered, or used for real-world prizes.

### Ads

Best ad policy:

- No ads during active turns.
- No ads that delay a player from returning to a live table.
- Use rewarded ads for optional cosmetic currency.
- Use interstitials only after match completion or on safe navigation moments.
- Members and remove-ads buyers get no forced ads.

## What Not To Monetize

Do not charge for:

- Legal move hints in ranked games.
- Better bots or easier opponents in ranked games.
- Extra time in competitive matches.
- Access to fair matchmaking.
- Hidden information, advanced hand reads, or opponent tracking.
- Cash prizes, wagered coins, buy-ins, sweepstakes entries, or prize pools.

These would either harm trust, create pay-to-win concerns, or move the product toward gambling/compliance risk.

## Best Business Model Recommendation

Use a hybrid model:

1. Free core play keeps liquidity high.
2. Membership monetizes committed players and friend-group hosts.
3. Cosmetics monetize identity.
4. Season pass monetizes recurring engagement.
5. Rewarded ads monetize non-spenders without interrupting games.
6. Club upgrades monetize community organizers later.

The best first paid product is not a season pass. It is **Domino Vibes Club** plus **Remove Ads Forever**. The app needs stable gameplay, table hosting, profiles, stats, and cosmetic inventory before a season pass can feel valuable.

## Suggested Monetization Rollout

### Monetization Sprint 1: Payments Foundation

- Add entitlement model: free, member, remove-ads owner.
- Add products in configuration, not hardcoded UI.
- Add account billing screen.
- Add receipt/webhook handling.
- Add "no gameplay advantage" policy copy.

### Monetization Sprint 2: No Ads and Private Hosting

- Add forced ads only at safe breaks for free users.
- Add no-ads membership.
- Add one-time remove-ads purchase.
- Add private table hosting limit for free users.
- Add unlimited private hosting for members.

### Monetization Sprint 3: Cosmetic Inventory

- Add inventory table.
- Add owned/equipped avatar frames, table felt, tile backs, and emotes.
- Add direct-purchase cosmetic packs.
- Add member monthly cosmetic grant.

### Monetization Sprint 4: Advanced Profiles and Replays

- Add extended match history for members.
- Add replay archive.
- Add advanced stats by mode.
- Add shareable recap cards.

### Monetization Sprint 5: Seasons

- Add season XP.
- Add free and premium tracks.
- Keep all rewards cosmetic.
- Add weekly challenges that reward play across modes.

### Monetization Sprint 6: Clubs

- Add club creation for members.
- Add club identity packs.
- Add club tables and club leaderboards.
- Add club-hosted events without cash prizes.

## Pricing Recommendation

Start simple:

- Free: $0.
- Domino Vibes Club: $4.99/month.
- Domino Vibes Club Annual: $39.99/year.
- Founder Annual during beta: $29.99/year.
- Remove Ads Forever: $9.99.
- Cosmetic packs: $1.99-$7.99.
- Season pass later: $7.99.
- Club identity pack later: $9.99-$19.99 one-time per club.

Why this price:

- It is close enough to competitor no-ads subscriptions to feel normal.
- It stays below broader board-game platform pricing.
- It gives annual members a meaningful discount.
- It keeps the app friendly to Caribbean/Latino family and casual audiences.
